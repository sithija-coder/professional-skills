Emotional Intelligence

Understanding how emotions shape thinking, relationships, and performance and why EQ is as critical as IQ in the workplace.

What Is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (EI) is the ability to perceive, understand, use, and manage emotions both in yourself and in others (Salovey & Mayer, 1990). In practical terms, emotionally intelligent individuals can motivate themselves, read social situations accurately, and build lasting relationships.

Perceive EmotionsRecognising emotions in yourself and others as a foundational awareness.
Understand EmotionsKnowing the patterns and behaviours linked to how emotions are expressed.
Use EmotionsHarnessing emotional states to enhance thinking, creativity, and focus.
Manage EmotionsRegulating your own emotions and positively influencing others' states.

IQ vs EQ

Both matter but for different reasons. IQ gets you into the room; EQ determines how effectively you perform once you're there.

IQ — Intelligence Quotient

  • Measures cognitive reasoning and problem-solving
  • Helps succeed academically and technically
  • Relatively fixed after adolescence
  • Strong predictor of academic performance

EQ — Emotional Quotient

  • Measures ability to understand and manage emotions
  • Helps navigate stress, conflict, and relationships
  • Can be developed and improved over time
  • Strong predictor of career and leadership success

Research shows that leaders with low EQ risk lower team engagement and higher staff turnover making emotional intelligence a critical leadership competency.

The Five EI Competencies (Goleman)

Self-AwarenessRecognising your own emotions, strengths, weaknesses, and how they affect others.
Self-RegulationControlling disruptive impulses; thinking before acting under pressure.
MotivationInternal drive to achieve beyond external rewards resilience and optimism.
EmpathyUnderstanding others' emotions and responding with care and consideration.
Social SkillsManaging relationships, inspiring others, and communicating effectively.

Aristotle's Challenge

"Anyone can become angry that is easy. But to be angry with the right person, to the right degree, at the right time, for the right purpose, and in the right way that is not easy."

This quote captures why EI is a lifelong practice. Emotional mastery is not about suppression it is about precision, context, and wisdom.
