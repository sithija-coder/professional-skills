Professional Responsibilities & Code of Ethics

Defining professionalism, understanding ethical frameworks, and recognising the responsibilities that computing professionals hold towards society.

Core Definitions

ProfessionA calling requiring specialised knowledge and training that provides meaningful services to society.
ProfessionalA person who possesses expert knowledge, works to standards, and accepts ethical responsibilities.
ProfessionalismThe behaviour and attitude of professionals commitment to quality, accountability, and ethical conduct.
EthicsA set of moral principles that govern behaviour and decision-making, particularly when interests compete.

Profession vs Job vs Career

Job

  • A task performed for monetary reward
  • Focus is on immediate income
  • May not require specialised training

Profession

  • Requires extended education and expertise
  • Governed by a code of ethics
  • Serves a broader social purpose

Key Ethical Responsibilities for Computing Professionals

Computing professionals hold significant responsibilities because their work directly affects people's data, privacy, security, and access to critical services.

Do No HarmEnsure systems and software do not cause harm to users, third parties, or society at large.
Honesty & TransparencyBe truthful about system capabilities, limitations, and risks to clients and stakeholders.
Respect for PrivacyProtect user data and uphold individuals' right to privacy in all professional activities.
FairnessEnsure systems are inclusive and do not embed bias or discriminatory outcomes.
AccountabilityAccept responsibility for your work, including errors, and take action to correct them promptly.
Intellectual PropertyRespect copyrights, licences, and ownership of software and creative works at all times.

My Reflection

Being technically skilled is necessary, but being a true professional means carrying ethical awareness into every decision. I want that to be a core part of my identity, not an afterthought.
