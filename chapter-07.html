Dining Etiquette & Table Manners

Navigating formal and informal dining with confidence from table settings and napkin use to the social awareness that defines a polished professional.

Why Dining Etiquette Matters

First impressions are formed within seconds, and dining situations business lunches, client dinners, networking events are prime professional arenas. Your behaviour at the table signals confidence, cultural awareness, and respect for others. These are qualities every employer and colleague notices, often before any work is discussed.

The BMW Rule & Table Reading

B  ·  M  ·  W

Bread on your left  ·  Meal in the centre  ·  Water on your right

Outside InUse utensils from the outermost inward as each course is served.
Napkin FirstUnfold and place on your lap immediately upon being seated.
Rest PositionFork and knife crossed - still eating. Parallel - finished.
Wait for HostDo not begin eating until the host starts or invites everyone to eat.

Napkin & General Manners

✅ DO

  • Place napkin on lap as soon as seated
  • Dab never wipe your mouth
  • Set napkin on chair when leaving temporarily
  • Chew with your mouth closed
  • Keep phone away during meals
  • Engage in polite, inclusive conversation

❌ DON'T

  • Tuck napkin into collar or waistband
  • Use it to blow your nose at the table
  • Talk with food in your mouth
  • Reach across others for items
  • Begin eating before the host
  • Stack or push plates away when done

My Reflection

Dining etiquette was the most unexpectedly practical chapter. The BMW rule alone changed how I approach every table. More importantly, I understood the deeper message good manners are about making others feel comfortable and valued, not following rules for their own sake. That principle applies far beyond the dining room.
