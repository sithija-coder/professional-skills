CV Writing

Crafting a compelling, tailored Curriculum Vitae that makes a strong first impression tructure, content, and what employers actually look for.

What Is a CV?

A Curriculum Vitae (CV) is a comprehensive document summarising your education, experience, skills, and achievements. It is your first opportunity to make an impression on a potential employer and plays a critical role in securing an interview. A well-crafted CV can set you apart; a poor one ends your application immediately.

Essential CV Components

Personal InfoFull name, email, phone, location, and optionally a LinkedIn or portfolio link.
Professional SummaryA concise 3–4 line overview of your goals, key skills, and value proposition.
Work ExperienceRoles, organisations, dates, and key achievements — using action verbs.
EducationDegrees, institutions, graduation dates, and notable academic achievements.
SkillsTechnical and soft skills relevant to the specific job being applied for.
AchievementsAwards, certifications, and measurable accomplishments that show your value.

Good Practices vs Common Mistakes

✅ Good Practices

  • Tailor the CV to each specific job
  • Keep it to 1–2 pages maximum
  • Use strong action verbs (led, built, achieved)
  • Quantify results where possible
  • Proofread carefully — zero errors
  • Use a clean, consistent format

❌ Common Mistakes

  • Using a generic, one-size-fits-all CV
  • Listing duties instead of achievements
  • Including irrelevant personal details
  • Inconsistent fonts or messy formatting
  • Spelling or grammatical errors
  • No clear professional summary

Tailoring & ATS

A tailored CV aligns directly with the specific role using keywords from the job description and demonstrating genuine research about the organisation. This also improves the chance of passing through Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS), the automated software that screens CVs before they reach a human recruiter.
