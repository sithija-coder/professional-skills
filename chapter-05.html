Interview Skills

Mastering verbal and non-verbal communication strategies, interview preparation, and the techniques that turn preparation into confident performance.

What Is a Job Interview?

A job interview is a structured conversation between an applicant and a hiring representative. Both parties assess each other the interviewer evaluates competencies, skills, and cultural fit; the candidate assesses whether the role and organisation match their goals. Preparation, communication, and confidence are the three pillars of a successful interview.

Communication in Interviews

Research shows that non verbal communication accounts for the majority of first impression impact making body language just as important as your spoken answers.

Eye ContactMaintain natural eye contact to signal confidence and engagement without staring.
PostureSit upright and open avoid crossing arms or slouching, which signals disinterest.
HandshakeA firm, professional handshake creates an immediate positive first impression.
Active ListeningNod, avoid interrupting, and pause before answering to show you process carefully.
Tone of VoiceSpeak clearly and at a steady pace avoid rushing when nervous.
Facial ExpressionA natural smile and engaged expression convey warmth and enthusiasm.

Best Interview Strategy

Build a personalised strategy by answering three key questions before entering the room:

What do I want the interviewer to know about me? Identify your top 3–4 strengths and prepare specific, concrete examples for each.
What weaknesses do I want to minimise? Acknowledge gaps honestly, then frame them around active steps you are taking to improve.
What do I need to know about this role and organisation? Research the company, culture, and sector thoroughly show genuine interest.

The STAR Method

The STAR method provides a clear structure for answering competency based questions concisely and convincingly:

STAR Framework

  • S — Situation: Set the scene and context of the example
  • T — Task: Describe your specific responsibility or challenge
  • A — Action: Explain the steps you took and why
  • R — Result: Share the outcome and what you learned

My Reflection

The mock interview revealed how different knowing and doing truly are. I tend to over explain when nervous, making answers unclear. Practising the STAR method gave me a structure that keeps responses concise and confident. I also became far more conscious of non-verbal signals a firm handshake and upright posture communicate professionalism before a word is spoken.
